Once a man can really gain the interest from learning, he sometimes would act like being controlled.

If one can works hard to the state of forgetting to sleep and eat, not caring about time’s fast flowing, it’s really natural and reasonable for him to acquire great success in study，isn’t it？

But, did he take delight in them，when he learned from teachers, reading and studying ancient Chinese books in a young age?

And was he really interested in those things when writing stereotyped essays and poems? No, he didn’t, I think.

I have come cross lots of young friends who are diligent in study and excellent in the test, but interestingly, their Chinese language abilities are not enough to support them to convey the information successfully to others. Even when they need to write a letter, it would be difficult to make the sentences fluent. And when being asked the reason of that, they would blurt out the old excuse that they take no delight in Chinese.

And they throw away those subjects they dislike, for the boredom of them, considering them as something of no use.

Feeling relaxed and pleased, twittering with each other, and having no sense of guilt, they really believe they are carrying the true interest-ism.

They haven’t yet realized there is no learning which appear uninteresting, because interest needs us to find them out.

If they learn under the guidance of wise teachers, inch by inch and step by step, and explore a new world gradually, they will be obsessed in learning.

A person with average gift owns enough learning ability to get done the study of basic subjects of arts and science, so that it shouldn’t be as a matter of course to say he can well master one subject but behave poorly in another.

Only idleness and willfulness can make one abandon himself in the disguise of interest, willingly but frustrated.

What you can learn from primary and secondary school is just some basic knowledge

Even during the college years, students can only learn rather rudimentary knowledge in class.

University is often called as the highest seat of learning, but it would cause misunderstanding among students for the simple reason that it seems there exists no learning out of university.

The institute in university is indeed the right place where students can promote preliminary knowledge acquisition, but even in this kind of environment, they can only study rudimentary knowledge, paying more attention to methods and experience gaining.

Learning knows no borders, so spending a whole life to study is still not enough. Therefore, even if ancient people are gray-haired , we can see they contribute their lives to reading and studying classics, which can enlighten us that in such learning process, people do gain full interest.

In the preliminary stage of learning—from primary school to university, we should pay more attention to discipline rather than advocating interest.

A reasonably arranged curriculum is something like a nutrition-balanced food cookbook, in which each item/element/constituent is beneficial and necessary, and in which the courses cannot be abolished or replaced.

The so-called electives only mean you have some limited options within the range of a certain item.

A well-educated person is like a well-trained drama actor.

During the training time, he has to strictly obey the discipline of theater.

He must learn attentively on singing, acting, and acrobatic techniques, and know well about how to play different roles.

learning with a solid foundation will provides us with lifelong benefits.

In the primary stage of learning, Chinese and Mathematics are the most important subjects, compared with others.

Chinese serves as a tool of reading and communication.

Mathematics is the best way of training for logical thinking.

Other subjects also have their own usage, so it’s hard to tell what is the best of them. For example, to consider Physical Education from another perspective/dimension, it cannot be more significant!

In a word, during the school years, we should put aside the interest for a moment, and patiently obey the discipline asked in school, then turn ourselves into excellent people.

It won’t be late to enjoy the interest of learning in the future.

But I still haven’t lost my belief—-the belief for life.

My life would never, come to an end, but I have no idea about what are waiting for me ahead.

To the things in the future, however, I have some predictions or guessing; for the simple reason that the passing years are not a silent mute, it can give us some implications.

Now here, I will show readers an illustration that records my life of more than 10 years of the past.

Although it just reflects a small part of life, we can see how the torrent of life, which is formed by love and hate,happiness and sorrow, flows down the river of life.

I’m not a preacher, so I cannot give you readers wise directions that are suitable for yourselves, but I believe you can find out the best answer in this book.

Some people say that there is no road originally, and that a road forms, just because of the increase of people walking on the ground.

Others believe that there at first does exists a road, so that lots of people would come onto it.

I do not want to judge who is right or wrong.

I am still young. I want to live on. And I want to conquer life.

I know the torrent of life would never stop at all, so let’s see where it will take me to!

Autumn, wherever it is, is always charming.

However, the autumn in Peiping usually appears clear, tranquil and sad.

In the long-distance journey from Hangzhou to Qingdao and from Qingdao to Beijing, I spend time coming here just to enjoy the air of the autumn in Beijing, the ancient capital.

In the southern areas, people can enjoy autumn too, but vegetation there defoliate slowly, the air is moist, and the sky is in light color with regular/more rain and occasional/less wind.

When walking on the street among the citizens in Suzhou, Shanghai, Hangzhou or Amoy, Hong kong, Guangzhou, I could only taste a little bit of coolness. As to the autumn’s flavour, color, mood and style, I cannot get enough of the season in southern areas.

It has been more than a decade since I last met autumn in Peiping.

When I am in the south, the arrival of each autumn would put me in the memory of reed catkins of Tao Ran pavilion kiosk, the shadow of willow trees of Diao Yu island, insect chirping of Western Hill, moonlight Yu Quan Shan , and reverberating bell of Tan Zhe temple.

Suppose you stay at home in Peiping, even just hire a decrepit house in the teeming imperial city to live in, when you get up every morning to make a cup of tea and sit down in the yard, you can also have a look at luscious sky perching very high, and have an appreciation of the sound from pigeons flying around the house.

When you look up from under the locust tree, and count every beam of sunlight leaking from the gaps between leaves or sit on the ruined wall towards morning glory silently, you can naturally notice the hints of autumn.

As to the variety of morning glory, in my view, the flower in blue or white color is the best for the purpose of admiring, the second is the violet black, and the third the pink.

And it’s better to put a few yellow grasses between morning glories to act as a foil to make it more beautiful.

The locust tree in Peiping which serves as a foil can also remind people of the arrival of autumn.

The yard every morning would be covered with some kind of flower-like pistil falling from the locust tree, but it’s definitely not any kind of flower.

When stepping on the ground this time, you will discover no sound or even smell, and you can only feel a little and soft sense of touch under your both feet.

After the cleaner sweeps the yard under the trees, on the ground there would form a picture painted by dust left by the besom, and leave countless lines. It looks fine and smooth, and also relaxing, and you can even feel a sense of loneliness somehow.

People of ancient times had said that a fallen leaf of Wutong tree can inform you of the arrival of autumn, which may be embodied in these places.

Cicada’s weak chirping in autumn is a characteristic of the Northern places of China, for the reason that trees grow everywhere in the city, and that the houses are so low that wherever you are, you can hear their singing easily.3

But in the south, to hear this, you have to go out to the countryside or mountains.

The shrill noise of cicada is the same with that of rats or cricket, and honestly they are much like an insect pet that lives in every family in Peiping.

Another thing to talk about is the rain in autumn, and it seems that in northern regions of China, it rains more uniquely, attractively and decisively.

At the moment when the heaven turns dark, a cool wind suddenly appears, and then it rains fast and heavily.

When the rain is over, the cloud gradually moves to the west, the sky turns blue again, and the sun comes out.

Dressed with a thick unlined black clothes or lined jacket, some idler of the city stand at the end of a bridge or under a tree with a fire tube in his mouth, when it stops raining. And if an acquaintance comes to him, they would communicate with a slight sign, using a casual and slow tone:

“Oh, the weather is so cool!”

“Can’t agree more! It is said that a rain of fall, a rain of chill.”
